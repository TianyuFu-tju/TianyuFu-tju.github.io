Author: Tianyu Fu

Process-based hydrodynamics-water quality models are powerful tools for unraveling complex cause-effect relationships in aquatic ecosystems. However, increasing model complexity introduces greater parametric uncertainty, which hinders simulation accuracy. Two key challenges persist in model calibration:

  1. the inefficiency and subjectivity of traditional trial-and-error methods, and
  2. the limited applicability of parameters calibrated using short time-series datasets to broader hydrological conditions.

To address these issues, autocalibration techniques enhance calibration efficiency, while employing longer time-series calibration datasets is expected to improve model adaptability across diverse hydrological conditions.

Professor Chen Zhang and PhD Student Tianyu Fu from the Department of Hydraulic Engineering at Tianjin University have independently developed EFDC-ACT, an autocalibration toolkit for the 3D hydrodynamics-water quality model EFDC. Their work demonstrates that calibrating water quality models with longer time-series datasets significantly improves performance across a wider hydrological variability and reveals parameter responses to different hydrologic conditions.

EFDC-ACT v1.0.0 Autocalibration Toolkit

EFDC-ACT v1.0.0 employs the caRamel algorithm for multi-objective iterative optimization of model parameters, providing users with iterative parameter sets, model evaluation metrics, and time-series visualizations. Case studies show that EFDC-ACT reduces pre- and post-processing time from nearly 2 hours manually to under 2 minutes, enabling fully automated calibration and dramatically improving efficiency.

Recalibration of Yuqiao Reservoir Water Quality Model

The original model (Zhang et al., 2013, https://doi.org/10.2166/hydro.2012.131) was calibrated solely using a dry-year dataset. This study recalibrated the model using a continuous decade-long hydrodynamics-water quality dataset encompassing wet, normal, and dry years. The recalibrated model achieved Kling-Gupta Efficiency (KGE) coefficients of 0.10 (Total Phosphorus), 0.30 (Chlorophyll-a), and 0.74 (Dissolved Oxygen), markedly improving upon the original KGEs of -1.60, -3.03, and -0.19, respectively. This confirms the critical role of broader hydrological calibration data in enhancing model accuracy.

Parameter Adaptation Insights for Precision Gains

Calibrations using single-year datasets (wet, normal, or dry) were compared to decadal strategy. The decadal calibration increased KGE for Total Phosphorus, Chlorophyll-a, and Dissolved Oxygen by up to 196%, 134%, and 202%, respectively. This improvement stems from parameters balancing adaptability across hydrological conditions. For instance, compared to dry-year calibration, the broad-variability strategy reduced the maximum algal growth rate by 46%, basal metabolism rate by 14%, and predation rate by 58%. This maintained Chlorophyll-a accuracy while preventing Dissolved Oxygen overestimation in other hydrologic years.

The EFDC-ACT significantly advances water quality modeling efficiency, reducing time and resource costs. Furthermore, this research demonstrates that utilizing calibration datasets with wider hydrological variability substantially improves model accuracy, providing valuable insights for dynamic parameter estimation strategies.
